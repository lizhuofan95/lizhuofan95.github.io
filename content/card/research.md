A computational sociologist of knowledge, power, organizations, and networks, I am most interested in how scientists and tech companies methodize and capitalize knowledge about human behavior at the intersection of big data, open science, and commercialization. 

To answer this question, my research intersects network analysis, sociology and history of science, organizational theory, economic sociology, cultural sociology, and computational social science. 

My dissertation examines how corporate participation in open science recasts the transnational, interorganizational techno-social-semantic network and temporalities of researchers in computer vision, a subfield of artificial intelligence that mobilizes massive human behavioral and biometric data and is changing human society in many ways. 

I am also broadly interested in methods for exploratory analysis and causal inference with large-scale text and network data.

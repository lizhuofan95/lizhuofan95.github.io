About Me

My name is 李卓凡 Zhuofan Li.1 I am currently a 5th-year PhD student in sociology at the University of Arizona.

I study how scientists and companies methodize and capitalize knowledge about human behavior in the age of big data, venture capital, and open science. To answer this question, my research intersects network analysis, sociology and history of science, organizational theory, economic sociology, and computational social science. I am also broadly interested in methodology, especially for exploratory analysis and causal inference with large-scale text and network data.

My dissertation draws on text and network data from ArXiv, GitHub, and in-depth interviews to examine how corporate participation in open science shapes the transnational, inter-organizational networks and careers in computer vision and natural language processing, two rapidly commercializing subfields of artificial intelligence that mobilize massive human behavioral and biometric data.

My other projects explore the coevolution of identities and categories in weakly institutionalized fields, temporality of capital conversion in the biomedical field, techno-social infrastructure of interdisciplinarity, case-oriented decomposition of structural topic models, and a machine learning approach to semantic networks and analyzing ethnographic data.

I grew up in Tangshan, a coal-mining and steel-making city in Northern China. Local street food is what I miss the most, but Tangshan is also known for China’ earliest efforts of state-led industrialization in the 1870s, earliest Marxism-inspired labor movements in the 1920s, deadliest earthquake of the 20th century in 1976, and most recent and perhaps influential case of gender-based violence in public spaces in 2022.

My family now lives in Beijing, where I went to public middle school and high school. I spent many weekends on competitive programming and thought I would go on to study computer science2, but later reading about Max Weber3 changed my mind. I attended Franklin & Marshall College, a private liberal arts college in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, where I was fortunate to study sociology, philosophy, and history with some of the best teachers. I started my PhD at the University of Arizona in 2018, where the training emphasizes methodological rigor and versatility. On unproductive days, I enjoy reading historians, philosophers, and programmers.

I am currently visiting the Institute for the Study of Societal Issues at UC Berkeley.

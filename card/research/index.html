Research

I am most interested in how organizations methodize and capitalize knowledge about human behavior.

My dissertation examines how corporate participation in open science recasts knowledge infrastructure in computer vision, and other subfields of computer science that mobilize a transnational network of scientific labor force and massive human behavioral and biometric data.

To answer this question, my research intersects network analysis, sociology and history of science, organizational theory, economic sociology, cultural sociology, and computational social science. I am also broadly interested in methods for exploratory analysis and causal inference on large-scale text and network data.
